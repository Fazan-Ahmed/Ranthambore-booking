Rules & Guidelines

Important Safari Instructions

Follow these guidelines for a safe and responsible tiger safari experience.

1
Carry Valid Photo IDAll visitors must carry an original government-issued photo ID (Aadhaar, Passport, Driving License). Children below 5 yrs allowed free.
2
Dress in Muted ColorsWear earthy, muted tones — olive, khaki, brown. Avoid white and bright colors that disturb wildlife and reduce sighting chances.
3
No Noise or Flash PhotographyMaintain silence inside the park. Flash photography is strictly prohibited. Use DSLR cameras without flash for wildlife shots.
4
Stay in the VehicleNever step out of the safari vehicle inside the park under any circumstances. This is non-negotiable for your safety.
5
No Plastic or LitteringCarrying plastic items, food packets, or beverages inside the national park is strictly prohibited by forest law.
6
Report 30 Mins EarlyArrive at the safari gate at least 30 minutes before departure. Late arrivals may forfeit their permit without refund.
7
No Feeding WildlifeFeeding or approaching any wildlife is strictly illegal. Maintain a safe distance and observe without interaction.

Frequently Asked Questions

Fill our booking form above or click the WhatsApp button. Share your name, date, zone preference, and number of guests. We'll confirm and send permits within 2 hours.

A Gypsy is a 6-seater open jeep that enters core zones (1–5) — ideal for intimate wildlife experiences and photography. A Canter is a 20-seater open bus that covers buffer zones (6–10). Gypsy gives better tiger sighting chances; Canter is more affordable.

Zones 3 and 4 (near Padam Lake and Rajbagh Lake) consistently offer the highest tiger sighting probability. Zone 1 and Zone 5 are also excellent options.

October to June is the best season. Summer (April–June) has the highest sighting rate as tigers come to water bodies. October–February offers pleasant weather. The park closes from July 1 to September 30 (monsoon season).

Location

Find Ranthambore National Park

Located in Sawai Madhopur, Rajasthan — 180 km from Jaipur, 400 km from Delhi.

By Train

Sawai Madhopur Railway Station is the nearest. Direct trains from Delhi, Jaipur, and Mumbai.

By Road

180 km from Jaipur (~3.5 hrs), 400 km from Delhi (~6 hrs) via NH-48 and NH-148B.

By Air

Jaipur Airport (JAI) is the nearest — 180 km away. Taxis available at the airport.
